From the 305 to Brickell: building wealth young

The moves that compound when you start early — no trust fund required.

By Dyrique Mendoza, Founder & Principal · Email
3 min read · Jun 2026
In brief
  • Wealth isn’t luck or a trust fund — it’s a sequence of moves that compound.
  • Get legit → fundable → funded → grow → protect. Start early and time does the heavy lifting.
  • The yacht and the jet are outcomes; the plan is boring, repeatable, and works at 16 or 26.

From the 305 to Brickell isn't just a drive up US-1. It's a mindset — the belief that the strategy the giants reserve for the biggest companies in the world belongs to the next generation too.

Wealth isn't luck, and it isn't a trust fund. It's a sequence of moves that compound: get legit, get fundable, get funded, grow it, protect it. Start early and time does the heavy lifting.

The yacht, the jet, the real estate — those are outcomes, not the plan. The plan is boring, repeatable, and it works whether you start at 16 or 26.

You don't have to wait your turn. You have something the giants don't: time, and a generation that looks like the future and is built for it.

The stakes. The median white U.S. household holds about $188,200 in wealth — roughly 8× the ~$24,000 of the median Black household (Federal Reserve). Starting early is how the next generation closes that gap.

Start with the fundamentals. The compounding takes care of the rest.
