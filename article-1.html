Miami Gardens

Closing the credit gap in Miami Gardens

Florida's largest majority-Black city — where money still isn't taught. The playbook that changes that.

Dyrique Mendoza · Founder & Principal · Jul 2026

Miami Gardens is the largest majority-Black city in Florida — home to Florida Memorial University, and part of a Miami youth ecosystem that includes the King Carter Youth Foundation, U-Turn Youth Consulting, and Barrington Irving's BITTS aviation academy. It's a community full of hustle, talent, and ambition. It's also a community where, for generations, the language of money was never taught in a classroom.

The credit gap isn't about effort or intelligence. It's about access and information. When no one shows you how business credit works, how a lender reads a file, or how to separate your personal and business finances, you start every venture a step behind — not because you're less capable, but because nobody handed you the map.

The gap they leave behind is the largest, most underserved market in America. That's exactly who we're built for.

That's the gap we exist to close — not with hype or "quick funding" promises, but with the same fundamentals a Brickell firm would charge five figures to teach: build a real entity, build its own credit, make it verifiable, and walk into a lender prepared.

The numbers. Miami Gardens is ~114,000 residents, ~60% Black, median household income $63,627 (U.S. Census). Nationally, Black-owned businesses are denied loans at 39% vs 18% for white-owned firms, and 55% of those denials cite a low credit score (Federal Reserve Small Business Credit Survey). The median white household holds ~$188,000 in wealth; the median Black household, ~$24,000 — an 8× gap (Federal Reserve / Hechinger Report).

The next generation in Miami Gardens doesn't need permission. It needs the playbook. Here's where it starts.
